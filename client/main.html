The Challenge

The Chacha (Channel Challenge) Team is a group of 6 students who are going to relay swim across the English Channel in August 2016. We will begin our crossing in Dover and finish in Calais. This covers a distance of approximately 35km in a straight line (~50km taking into account currents). Our aim is to raise money for Obstetric Fistula.

Channel Swimming Association Rules

Swimmers:
  • Must swim alternatively for one hour.
  • Must rotate in the same order.
  • Cannot wear a wetsuit.
  • Must pass a 2 hour qualifier in water below 15.5 degrees.
  • Can only spend 10 minutes on French land upon arrival due to border control regulations.

The English Channel:

  • The English Channel is approximately 35 km long
  • In August the temperature of the water is about 15°C
  • Certain areas are prone to strong currents
  • Swimmers often get stung by jellyfish
  • It is the world’s busiest seaway
  • More people have climbed Mount Everest than swam the Channel

The Training:

  • Outdoor training in lake geneva to get used to the cold
  • Indoor training, at the moment 15km per week but this amount will gradually increase as we get closer to the
    • date of the crossing
  • Night swims in the lake as some of us will have to swim at night in the Channel
  • Our coaches are Kevin Murphy, the ‘King of the Channel’, who has crossed the Channel 34 times, which is more
    • than any other man in history, and Kathy Batts, who has relay swam the Channel 14 times

The Charity - Hamlin Fistula Ethiopia

http://hamlinfistula.org

Obstetric Fistula is a condition, which affects 2 million women in the world. These women can be saved with a small operation of $600. We encourage you to donate, as any small amount will make a massive difference!

The costs for the surgery, three weeks of care in a hospital and rehabilitation are only US$600. Only $600 to give a woman her life back.

80% of the money raised will go to the Hamlin Fistula Hospital in Ethiopia, the largest hospital in the world that solely treats this condition, the remaining 20% will go to the Mercy Women’s Hospital in Ghana, where many women, who suffer from obstetric fistula, are waiting for the funds for their surgery.

The eradication of Obstetric Fistula has now also become one of the UN’s goals.

Ottavia:If one word could describe me it would be adventurous. Whether it is hiking for 8 days, canoeing down a river, traveling to a foreign country or swimming the channel, I throw myself head and shoulders into every adventure no matter how big or small. For the last few years I have been looking for the ultimate challenge that tested me both mentally and physically whilst at the same time reaching out to the global community. As a dedicated competitive swimmer for the last 8 years, who trains 5-6 times a week, by finding something that uses the skills I have built up, allows me to help other whilst pursuing a passion. When swimming the channel as a relay to raise money of obstetric fistula came up, the seed was planted towards this ultimate challenge. And so the adventure begins...
